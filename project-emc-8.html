EMC 8

Currently under construction: a 300-ton steel structure across three floors and nearly 8,000 m² — with a completely free-span top floor.

Scope

EMC 8 is a full EPC turnkey delivery for Elite Merchandising Company in the Industrial Area of 6th of October City — a three-floor industrial facility spanning roughly 8,000 m², carried on a 300-ton steel structure and purpose-designed for fabric-industry manufacturing. Construction began in 2025 and is progressing toward completion in 2026, with Current handling engineering, procurement, and construction end to end.

The highlight of the advanced design is the top floor, engineered with a complete free span: no internal columns, no structural impediments — just uninterrupted production space offering ultimate operational flexibility. Achieving a clear span of this scale on the uppermost level of a multi-storey steel building demands precise structural engineering and disciplined erection sequencing, and the project stands as a working showcase of Current's expertise in large-scale steel construction and complex multi-storey industrial delivery.

Challenge

Deliver a three-floor, ~8,000 m² manufacturing facility whose top floor must remain entirely column-free — a demanding structural brief on a working industrial site.

Solution

A 300-ton engineered steel structure with long-span framing on the upper level, executed under a single EPC turnkey contract covering design through erection.

Result

A fabric-industry facility taking shape on schedule for 2026, with a fully free-span top floor giving the owner maximum layout flexibility for production lines.
