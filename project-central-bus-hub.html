Central Bus Station

24,000 m² of fast-track construction — soaring 11 m ceilings, 12 m spans, and 1.8 m circular fair-faced columns.

Scope

Delivered for the Egyptian Army in the New Administrative Capital on a fast-track programme through 2020–2021, this 24,000 m² transport hub is defined by its structural ambition: rows of circular fair-faced reinforced concrete columns, each 1.8 m in diameter, rising through 11 m ceiling heights to carry post-tensioned concrete solid slabs across clear 12 m spans. Every column face was cast as finished architecture — no cladding, no correction — demanding millimetre-level formwork discipline across the entire civil and concrete-structures package.

Below grade, the system rests on massive independent foundations tied together through a network of grade beams, topped with precision-crafted fair-faced slabs on grade that double as the station's finished floor structure. Compressing a structure of this scale into a fast-track window meant sequencing foundation pours, post-tensioning cycles, and formwork rotations in continuous overlap — a programme executed without compromising the exposed-concrete quality the design demanded.

Challenge

Deliver 24,000 m² of exposed structural concrete — 1.8 m circular fair-faced columns, 12 m spans, 11 m ceilings — on a fast-track programme where every cast surface is the finished product.

Solution

Post-tensioned solid slabs on precision fair-faced columns, founded on massive independent footings linked by grade beams, with formwork and tensioning cycles sequenced in continuous overlap.

Result

A landmark transport hub for the New Administrative Capital, completed within the compressed 2020–2021 window with architectural-grade concrete throughout.
