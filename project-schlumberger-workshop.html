Schlumberger Workshop

A 7,500 m² state-of-the-art workshop in the Libyan desert — overhead cranes, drive-through bays, and double-floor testing labs, delivered EPC.

Scope

Deep in Libya's Al Wahat Desert at the 103 Oilfield, we delivered a 7,500 m² maintenance workshop for Schlumberger on a full EPC basis. The building is a pre-engineered, fully insulated steel structure set on robust concrete footings and a 250 mm reinforced concrete slab — engineered to carry heavy oilfield equipment and constant vehicle traffic. Drive-through maintenance bays with manual and electrical roll-up doors run the length of the facility, served by overhead cranes for lifting drilling and testing equipment directly at the workstations.

Beyond the structure itself, the scope covered the complete industrial fit-out: comprehensive workshop lighting and compressed-air networks, galvanized electrical conduits and cable trays feeding calibrated MDB and distribution boards, and advanced firefighting, alarm, and sanitary systems throughout. A double-floor design integrates testing laboratories, administration offices, and storage areas within the same envelope — giving Schlumberger's field teams a single, self-contained base of operations in one of the most remote working environments in North Africa.

Challenge

Build a fully serviced industrial workshop in a remote desert oilfield, where every material, crane, and crew had to be mobilized over long desert supply lines.

Solution

A pre-engineered insulated steel building erected fast on heavy concrete footings and a 250 mm slab, with all MEP networks — power, air, lighting, fire — installed as one coordinated EPC package.

Result

A 7,500 m² double-floor facility combining drive-through crane bays, testing labs, offices, and storage — handed over ready for round-the-clock oilfield operations.
