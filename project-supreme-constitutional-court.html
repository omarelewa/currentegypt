Supreme Constitutional Court

An Egyptian landmark on the Nile — ancient Egyptian symbolism embodied in modern architecture and engineering.

Scope

The Supreme Constitutional Court on the Maadi Korniche in Cairo began with an international architectural design competition — won in collaboration with Arch. Ahmed Mito. From that winning concept, Current developed the full tender and construction documents, translating the design's ancient Egyptian symbolism into buildable modern architecture. The building houses the court's administrative body alongside its court and ceremonial halls — a national landmark on the Nile symbolizing the respect of law.

From 1995 to 1999, and jointly with Sabbour Consultants, Current carried complete architectural construction supervision over works executed by Arab Contractors — leading design development and on-site problem-solving throughout construction, and coordinating the architecture with every structural and MEP requirement so the landmark was delivered exactly as conceived.

Challenge

Turn a competition-winning concept rich in ancient Egyptian symbolism into a fully engineered national landmark meeting every structural and MEP requirement.

Solution

Full tender and construction documentation, then complete architectural supervision with Sabbour Consultants — design development and problem-solving alongside Arab Contractors on site.

Result

A landmark on the Nile delivered in four years, housing the court's administrative body, court and ceremonial halls — an enduring symbol of the respect of law.
