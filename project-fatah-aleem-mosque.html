Al Fattah El Aleem Mosque — Celebration Halls

A pivotal early build of the New Administrative Capital — celebration halls with high ceilings and wide spans, delivered on an exceptionally tight timeframe.

Scope

Commissioned by the New Administrative Capital Co. (ACUD) in 2015–2016, the celebration halls of the Al Fattah El Aleem Mosque were among the earliest structures raised in the new capital east of Cairo. Current delivered project management, earthworks, the complete concrete structure and full exterior and interior finishings — a robust reinforced-concrete frame incorporating underground parking and comprehensive service areas, with spacious halls of high ceilings and wide spans erected within an exceptionally tight timeframe despite the site's remote location.

The finishing package matches the landmark's ambition. Facades integrate GRC elements with marble, precision CNC steel fabrications and cement board, crowned by decorative domes atop steel structures, heavy-duty wooden doors and double-glazed aluminium. Inside, classic wooden and GRC wall panels meet ornate marble and wooden flooring, while GRC false ceilings conceal a dense network of modern MEP and HVAC systems serving the halls below.

Challenge

Erect wide-span, high-ceiling celebration halls at a remote greenfield site on an exceptionally tight programme.

Solution

Single-team delivery of earthworks, concrete structure and finishings, with GRC, CNC steel and marble packages sequenced in parallel.

Result

A landmark of the New Administrative Capital completed on schedule, with underground parking and full MEP/HVAC integration.
