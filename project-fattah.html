Fattah Project

Construction and finishing works for a landmark public building in Egypt, delivered to an exacting standard.

Scope

The Fattah project is a landmark public building where the finishing quality carries the architecture. Current executed the full construction and finishing package — structural works through to final surfaces — under a single coordinated delivery team, so detailing decisions on site never drifted from the design intent.

Because landmark buildings are judged at arm's length, the finishing programme was sequenced trade by trade: substrates verified before claddings, wet works closed out before fine finishes, and every visible junction inspected against benchmark samples approved early in the job. The building was handed over complete, consistent, and to the standard the brief demanded.

Challenge

Deliver a high-visibility landmark building where finishing quality would be scrutinised on every surface and junction.

Solution

A single team ran construction and finishing works in a coordinated sequence, with benchmark samples and trade-by-trade sign-off governing quality.

Result

A landmark public building completed and handed over to a high, uniform finishing standard.
