Electromechanical Works (MEP)

Integrated HVAC, fire protection, and electrical systems with tight cost control, delivered by self-performed teams.

Overview

Current installs the full electromechanical scope as one coordinated package: HVAC, electrical works and power distribution, fire-fighting and fire-detection systems, plumbing and drainage, and building controls. Because our crews are self-performed, mechanical, electrical, and fire disciplines are coordinated from shop drawings through installation — routing dense concealed MEP and HVAC services behind GRC false ceilings, as delivered in the Al Fattah El Aleem halls, without clashes or rework.

The same teams handle demanding industrial specifications: compressed-air networks, galvanized conduits and cable trays run to calibrated MDBs and distribution boards for the Schlumberger and FOX9 workshops, and custom medium- and low-voltage installations built to each client's voltage and frequency requirements. In-house QA/QC governs every stage, HSE supervision covers all site works, and every system is tested, commissioned, and handed over with complete documentation.

What's included

  • HVAC & ventilation
  • Electrical & power distribution
  • Fire detection & protection
  • Plumbing & drainage
  • Controls & commissioning
  • Compressed-air networks & industrial utilities
  • Galvanized conduits, cable trays & calibrated MDB / distribution boards
  • Custom MV/LV installations to client voltage & frequency specs

How we deliver

Step 01

Coordinate & engineer

We review the design, produce coordinated shop drawings across HVAC, electrical, fire, and plumbing, and resolve routing clashes before a single conduit is fixed — critical for dense concealed services above false ceilings.

Step 02

Procure & prefabricate

Equipment, panels, and materials are procured to approved submittals. Distribution boards and MDBs are built and calibrated to the client's voltage and frequency specifications before they reach site.

Step 03

Install self-performed

Our own crews run ductwork, piping, cable trays, and conduits under in-house QA/QC and HSE supervision — one accountable team across every discipline, sequenced with the civil and finishing trades.

Step 04

Test, commission & hand over

Every system is pressure-tested, energized, balanced, and commissioned against the specification, then handed over with as-built drawings, test certificates, and O&M documentation.
