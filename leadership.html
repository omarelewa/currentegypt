Leadership

The people accountable for your delivery.

This is the team that signs off on your project. Between them they bring military-grade governance, decades in oil and gas, and engineers who came up through the site. Every one of them answers for the work.

Chairman

Maj. Gen. Mohamed Abdelaziz

Major General Mohamed Abdelaziz spent his career in military command before he came to Current. That's where he learned to run large, complicated organisations and keep them moving. He now guides the company across Egypt, Libya and Saudi Arabia.

As Chairman he sets the direction. He holds the line on governance and finances, keeps a close eye on how projects are run, deals with the people who matter, and looks for the next market worth entering. He also drives the company's social responsibility work and how it handles risk.

Strategic governanceRisk managementRegional expansion
Chief Executive Officer

Eng. Mohamed Ragab

Eng. Ragab has run Current's operations since 2020. He has more than 18 years behind him in oil and gas, manufacturing and civil infrastructure, and he has managed multi-site industrial programmes across Egypt and the GCC. That means the whole job, from early planning and procurement through QA/QC and HSE right up to handover.

He has worked on oil and gas facilities in Libya, Sonatrach/Medgaz pipeline-related plants in Algeria, manufacturing operations in Turkey, and mixed contracts in Egypt. Under him, Current built the EMC2, EMC5, EMC7 and EMC8 facilities, and since 2023 has delivered critical infrastructure packages in Riyadh with government authorities.

18+ yearsOil & gas deliveryEgypt · GCC · North Africa
Construction Director

Eng. Mohamed Shelbaya

Eng. Shelbaya is good at the thing most people struggle with: getting the site and the technical office to work off the same page. He plans ahead, handles the contracts, and does the value engineering that turns hard drawings into something a crew can actually build.

He runs projects from start to finish. Design coordination, procurement, cost control, site supervision, QA/QC and HSE all sit with him, and he builds site teams that deliver on factories, warehouses, office buildings and infrastructure packages.

Site + technical officeQA/QC & HSEValue engineering
Engineering Consultant

Dr. Ahmed Baibars

Dr. Baibars is General Manager of ALNOOR Engineering Design & Consultant. He has more than 35 years in architectural and structural design for concrete and steel work across Egypt, and he leads teams from design through tender documentation, feasibility and on-site supervision to international standards.

His work covers pharmaceutical plants, food and chemical manufacturing facilities, warehouse complexes, schools, mosques, water and oil tanks, and mixed-use towers across Egypt.

35+ years designConcrete & steelInternational standards
Branch Manager, Saudi Arabia

Eng. Arafat Matar

Eng. Matar holds a B.Sc. in Architectural Engineering from Cairo University and runs Current's Saudi operations. He looks after the harder industrial and administrative jobs, including the MC2, MC5, MC7 and MC8 facilities and several factory expansions.

Earlier in his career he worked on the New Capital Central Bus Terminal, the R1 District, Resort 5 in Al Galala, and the Al-Fattah Al-Aleem Halls. He knows tender documentation, authority coordination, cost and schedule control, and quality assurance. He's IPMA-certified.

KSA operationsIPMA certifiedIndustrial & administrative
Administrative Director

Eng. Omar Ahmed

Eng. Omar runs administration, procurement and operations governance. His job is to keep the back office tight so the site teams aren't held up waiting on paperwork or materials.

Before Current he was Supply Chain and Procurement Manager at Procter & Gamble, where he handled tough contract negotiations and built up a network of vendors he could rely on. He now uses data dashboards and analytics to keep the company accountable and its decisions grounded in real numbers.
