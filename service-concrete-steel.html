Concrete & Steel Structures

Architectural-grade concrete and structural steel executed to accelerate schedules.

Overview

We build the structural backbone of demanding projects — reinforced-concrete frames, fair-face architectural concrete, and heavy structural steel — with our own crews, formwork, and erection teams. Our fair-face work is executed with advanced scaffolding and engineered formwork systems that compress cycle times without sacrificing finish: at Central Bus Station we cast 1.8 m-diameter circular fair-faced columns supporting post-tensioned solid slabs, delivered as exposed architecture straight off the form.

On the steel side, we fabricate and erect structures from pre-engineered insulated buildings with overhead cranes (Schlumberger, FOX9 workshops) to 250-ton frames for Adidas and Calvin Klein, 16 m-high columns at MEDCO, and a 300-ton three-floor structure with a completely free-span top floor at EMC 8. Every pour and every bolt-up runs through in-house QA/QC — rebar inspection, cube testing, survey control, torque verification — under a strict HSE regime.

What's included

  • Reinforced concrete frames & cores
  • Engineered formwork & falsework
  • Structural steel fabrication & erection
  • Precast & tilt-up elements
  • Post-tensioning & specialist concrete
  • Fair-face architectural concrete
  • Pre-engineered steel buildings & crane systems
  • Heavy lifts, long spans & free-span structures

How we deliver

Step 01

Structural planning

We review the design, agree tolerances and finishes, and engineer the formwork, scaffolding, and erection methodology around the programme.

Step 02

Fabrication & formwork

Steel is detailed and fabricated to shop drawings while formwork systems are assembled and trial-fitted — with material certificates and weld QA logged throughout.

Step 03

Casting & erection

Self-performed crews execute rebar, pours, post-tensioning, and steel erection in controlled cycles, with survey checks and cube tests at every stage.

Step 04

Inspection & handover

Torque verification, alignment surveys, and finish inspections close out each element before we hand over a documented, as-built structure.
